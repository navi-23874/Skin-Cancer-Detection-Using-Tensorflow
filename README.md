# Skin-cancer-detection-using-Tensorflow-
Skin cancer is one of the most common cancers worldwide, and I recognize that its early detection is crucial for improving survival rates. In this project, I developed a deep learning–based skin cancer classification model using Convolutional Neural Networks (CNNs). Traditional diagnostic methods, such as biopsies and manual examinations, are often time-consuming and subjective. My CNN-based approach automates the classification process, allowing for faster and more accurate detection of skin lesions.

I trained the model on real-world datasets like HAM10000 to ensure robustness and reliability in identifying various types of skin cancer. I evaluated the model’s performance using key metrics such as accuracy, precision, recall, and F1-score, and compared it with conventional machine learning techniques to highlight improvements in classification efficiency. With further enhancements, I believe this AI-driven system can assist dermatologists, minimize diagnostic errors, and make early skin cancer detection more accessible and effective.
